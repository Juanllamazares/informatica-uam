Interesting Facts About Kentucky

Kentucky is one of four commonwealth states in the United States of America, and is usually associated with the Southern states - despite its cultural and geographic relationship to the Midwest. Kenucky was formed by spliting from Virginia in 1792, and is the 15th state to join the union. It the 37th largest state by land area, and the 26th largest in terms of population.

Kentucky is widely known as the "Bluegrass State," and is also known for distilleries, horses, horse racing, bluegrass music, tobacco and college basketball. In higher education, Kentucky supports a wide array of colleges and universities. Please browse the list below for more information.
