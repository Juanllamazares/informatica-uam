Deborah Mitchell


Seniors' Health/Medicare Feature Writer

My writing career probably started when I was about 7 years old when I wrote and illustrated a story about a man from outerspace and bound my efforts with cardboard covered with hideous yellow and orange contact paper. Since then my interests have turned to health, nutrition, medicine, the environment, animals, and biographies, and the volume of my work has followed that order. I have authored, coauthored, and ghostwritten more than 40 books in the health/nutrition/medical field, and they have been published by various houses, including St. Martin's, Dell, Prentice Hall, Barnes & Noble, Wiley, Avon, HarperCollins, and others.

I have always had a special interest in geriatric medicine, but for many years that interest manifested itself solely in the writing arena. Then at one point I became a Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA), which allowed me to work "in the field" and experience first-hand many of the health challenges seniors face every day. Although I no longer work as a CNA, I believe the work strengthened my understanding of what it means to grow older in today's society.

I was fortunate to be the Senior Editor for Environmental Protection for Charity Guide, which allowed me to write many articles on the environment and how we can collectively do small things to better our world. Although I'm a bit shy in the biography arena, it is one of my goals to write a biography one day, and in the meantime I have written several profiles for publication.

I also season my writing schedule with pieces I have written and continue to write for various websites with topics ranging from nutritional supplements to gene therapy to baby sleep.
