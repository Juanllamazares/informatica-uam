Golfweeks Best Casino Courses
By BRADLEY S. KLEIN
Senior Writer

Golfweek has found one way to reduce the risk inherent in casino gambling. While we claim no expertise in regards to card games, roulette wheels or gaming machines, we do have insight on matters of fine golf course architecture. Thats illustrated by our 2008 list of Golfweeks Best Casino Courses.

Shadow Creek Golf Club in North Las Vegas, a 1990 design by Tom Fazio, heads our list. Its the first of six Nevada courses in our rankings, making the Silver State our front-runner when it comes to betting on great golf. Neighboring Arizona follows with five ranked courses, the best being We-Ko-Pa Golf Clubs Saguaro Course (No. 3) in Fountain Hills.

While Shadow Creek has a contrived, though compelling, glitz, Saguaro is all about subtlety.

Here, traditionalists Bill Coore and Ben Crenshaw created a walkable, old-fashioned course that stays true to the desert look by avoiding showy water features.

Our choices are not limited to the Southwest. Indeed, casino-related golf is firmly embedded in the staid old Northeast. Turning Stone Resort in Verona, N.Y., sports three modern courses: Atunyote (No. 17), Kaluhyat (No. 18) and Shenendoah (No. 23). At one of the countrys largest gaming resorts, Foxwoods Casino in North Stonington, Conn., the Rees Jones-designed Lake of Isles North Course (No. 12) has drawn attention for its bold use of exposed rock and the resulting dramatic forced carries on tee shots.

Most of our courses are of recent vintage, but there also are two classic designs that have benefited from recent facelifts. The French Lick (Ind.) Resorts Ross Course (No. 14) is a newly refurbished version of the 1917 original, replete with some of the boldest greens that Ross ever created. And our No. 6 layout, Atlantic City Country Club in Northfield, N.J., is a Tom Doak-designed partial rerouting and restoration of William Flynns 1923 original. In both cases, the older design heritage has been used to make classic golf amenable to a modern clientele.
