Latino organization gets "Environmental Hero Award"

Latino organization gets 'Environmental Hero Award'
by Sonia Gandara, Chicago Latino Community Examiner January 6

State officials presented "Environmental Hero Awards" to 26 individuals and groups who have shown commitment to environmental health and protection.

Lt. Gov., Pat Quinn, said those who received the awards have done a lot of work to make Illinois a cleaner and better place to live.

Among those being honored is the Little Village Environmental Justice Organization, which works on issues in and around the Little Village community.

Kimberly Wasserman, coordinator for the LVEJO, said that it was an honor to receive the award along with other organizations who work on the environment.

"This award shows that Illinois is rich with individuals and organizations who take their duty of protecting our environment to heart through struggling to keep our forest preserves open, protecting our eagles, teaching about the environment, retrofitting our places of worship and creating urban agriculture. In this time when our state is facing much scrutiny, it is positive to hear about this amazing work, and be awarded by a lieutenant governor who cares and appreciates our work and our state," said Wasserman.
