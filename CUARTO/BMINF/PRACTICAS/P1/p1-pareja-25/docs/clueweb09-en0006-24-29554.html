Getting Organized: Start with the Kitchen Cabinets

by Heather Tyree
on 01/07/09 at 12:12 PM

It's a brand new year and I have a shiny set of resolutions, most of them dusted off from the middle of last January. Near the top of my list: Get organized. Let me remind you that I live in Manhattan and spend an inordinate amount of time in my kitchen, which, to be generous, is about the size of shoe box. I will also admit that I am more than a little obsessed with kitchen gadgets and appliances. Unfortunately, given my current system of using my microwave for food storage and heaping Zip Lock bags full of dry goods onto the highest shelves of my cabinets, it is often difficult to locate a needed ingredient without a lengthy search and the help of a sturdy chair. But I found a solution....

About once a year, I resolve to sort through my cabinets, tossing out anything past its prime, consolidating like products that have languished too long alone, organizing flours, legumes, spices, and oils into some semblance of functioning, usable chaos that will survive for a few months. Clearly, what I really need is a long-lasting solution. This modular container set, new from OXO, comes with 11 airtight containers that will seal in and keep my dry foods fresh. Some are small enough for my leftover cinnamon, others large enough for a full five-pound bag of flour or tall enough to comfortably hold spaghetti. The airtight seal is created with one touch of the button that also doubles as a handle for fuss-free reaching and pouring. The containers are made from a clear, BPA-free plastic that will make it easy for me to keep tabs on which staples I am running low on and, with their square and rectangular shapes, they will stack neatly without taking up a lot of space in my cupboards. I'm sold.

11 Piece Good Grips POP Container Set, $99.99; oxo.com
