Molly Mitchell  
Snap to It (Evening Gown)
variable
snaps, twill tape
2006
 
Artist Statement
Fashion and architecture are both concerned with structuring body-space. But beyond creating a physical object for a body to inhabit, a garment and a building are both constructed to facilitate certain forms of movement while restricting others – they shape how we think and move. My work blends performance and costuming with site-specific installations to explore the interconnections between clothing, body and built space. The clothing systems I create out of materials such as rubber and snap tape, eye bolts and chain links make use of both sewing and hardware connectors to allow points of contact and rupture, tension and release to be established between a performer and the space around her. By giving concrete form to a largely intangible relationship between the body in motion and its environment, I strive to make visible the invisible, felt relationships between bodies and their culturally constructed surroundings that sometimes restrain them, sometimes free them and sometimes do both at once.
